## Part 3: PMF Validation Summary

### What this landscape tells us

**The multi-entry coordination pain point is confirmed real.**

Multiple independent data points validate this:

1. **PoolGenius explicitly writes about "portfolio management" for multi-entry survivor players.** Their guide covers diversification, EV, and pick distribution across entries. They know this problem exists. Their tool addresses it generically, not personally.

2. **DraftKings allows up to 150 entries in their Main Event survivor contest.** FanDuel allows multi-entry. ESPN allows 25 entries. Zero of these platforms provide coordination tooling. The hosting infrastructure was built to serve multi-entry players. The strategy infrastructure was not.

3. **SurvivorSweat explicitly prices multi-entry discounts** and serves the highest-stakes multi-entry community (Circa Survivor). Their customers are paying $1,000+/entry × up to 10 entries with no portfolio management tool beyond gut and spreadsheet.

4. **The Circa Survivor contest** ($15M+ prize, 10-entry maximum, $1,000/entry) is the most financially committed survivor pool on earth. The community around it (SurvivorSweat, CircaSurvivorTV) is the densest concentration of SurvivorPulse's exact ICP.

5. **PoolCrunch's "Portfolio Optimal Path"** confirms that the market wants cross-entry coordination — but their implementation is per-entry-group, not personalized. SurvivorPulse's structural moat (tracking each user's specific entry history across all pools and delivering individualized CMEA) is a genuine capability gap that PoolCrunch and PoolGenius cannot close without compromising their subscriber-population model.

### Market size signal

This is a concentrated but high-value market, not a mass market:

- **Primary ICP (3-10+ entries):** Players in DK/FanDuel $1.5M Main Events, Circa Survivor, and large private pools. Likely tens of thousands of users nationally, spending $100-$10,000+ per season on entry fees.
- **Secondary ICP (1-2 entries, scaling to 3+):** Players using PoolCrunch, ESPN Eliminator, and Sleeper survivor who are currently single-entry but strategically minded enough to scale.
- **Total addressable market:** The survivor pool market has grown significantly; estimates put total annual participation in the millions but the "serious tool user" segment is smaller. PoolGenius notes their subscribers (who tend to be serious players) commonly have 10, 20, or more entries.

**Revenue implication:** A player running 10 DK Main Event entries at $50/entry = $500/season in entry fees. They will pay $20-100/month for a tool that materially improves their coordination. This is a B2C SaaS product with willingness-to-pay correlated to total entry fee spend.
